# Acceptable Use and Privacy Policy Notes

## Acceptable Use Policy

An Acceptable Use Policy explains how users are allowed to use organizational systems, accounts, networks, devices, and data.

### Common Policy Statements

#### 1. Use systems only for approved purposes

Users should use company systems for authorized work and approved activities.

#### 2. Do not share passwords

Users should keep passwords private and protect account credentials.

#### 3. Do not disable security controls

Users should not turn off antivirus, firewall, endpoint protection, logging, or other required safeguards.

#### 4. Do not install or spread malicious software

Users should not create, install, download, or distribute malware, hacking tools, or unauthorized software.

#### 5. Follow copyright and software licensing rules

Users should not install pirated software or copy protected data without permission.

#### 6. Do not harass, threaten, or perform illegal activity

Organizational systems should not be used for harassment, abuse, discrimination, fraud, or illegal actions.

### Why It Matters

Acceptable use policies set user expectations and support enforcement when systems are misused.

## Privacy Policy

A Privacy Policy explains how an organization collects, uses, stores, protects, shares, and deletes personal information.

### Common Policy Statements

#### 1. What information is collected

The policy should explain what types of personal data are collected.

#### 2. Why information is collected

The policy should explain the business or legal reasons for collecting data.

#### 3. How information is used

The policy should explain how collected data supports services, operations, security, or compliance.

#### 4. How information is shared

The policy should explain whether information is shared with vendors, partners, law enforcement, or other third parties.

#### 5. How information is protected

The policy should describe security safeguards used to protect personal information.

#### 6. How long information is retained

The policy should explain retention periods or criteria for deleting data.

#### 7. User rights and choices

The policy should explain whether users can access, correct, delete, or restrict use of their information.

### Why It Matters

Privacy policies build trust, support legal compliance, and help users understand how their personal information is handled.

---

## Security Governance Connection

Acceptable use policies guide user behavior. Privacy policies guide data handling. Technical controls, such as Group Policy and mobile device security settings, help enforce these expectations.
